The Stylistic Conventions of Modernist Architecture:

  • Embraces Rectangular forms with clean lines
  • Open floor plans
  • Large, horizontal windows or curtain glass
  • Highlights a connection between the interior and exterior
  • Utilizes Steel, glass, and reinforced concrete among the most prominent building materials

An Overview On Modernism:

Modernist or “modern” architecture precedes, encompasses, and outlasts brutalism among several other architectural styles of the 20th century. Modernist architecture or rather the totality of the modernist art movement in general was an international effort to reject traditional architectural conventions and explore the limitations of aesthetics and engineering. Much of modernist architecture is rather conceptual, many of the greatest modernist architects such as Frank Lloyd Wright or Le Corbusier designed to not only build a structure but to satisfy a greater philosophical thesis. This desire to expand what architecture could be resulted in many breakthroughs in engineering utilized even today, such as reinforced concrete, allowing architects to design larger spaces without the need for many columns and other forms of structural support. Modernism is responsible for the emergence of early skyscrapers and other large-scale structures previously thought impossible in the decades preceding it.
